Tacos

Description

Tacos are a beloved Mexican dish consisting of small, folded tortillas filled with a variety of flavorful ingredients. Typically made with soft corn or flour tortillas, tacos are often filled with seasoned meat such as beef, chicken, pork, or fish, then topped with fresh garnishes like lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cheese, and salsa. The combination of warm, savory fillings and cool, crisp toppings creates a satisfying and customizable meal enjoyed around the world.

From traditional street-style tacos topped simply with cilantro and onions to creative modern versions featuring grilled shrimp, roasted vegetables, or spicy sauces, tacos are endlessly versatile. They can be made as light snacks or hearty meals, and their appeal lies in the ability to mix and match flavors to suit any taste. Whether served at a family dinner, a backyard party, or a late-night food truck stop, tacos are a fun, flavorful, and timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef, chicken, or pork
  • 1 packet taco seasoning (or 2 tbsp homemade blend)
  • 8–10 small tortillas (corn or flour)
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • Salsa, sour cream, and guacamole for topping

Steps

  1. Cook the Meat — Heat a pan over medium heat. Add the ground meat and cook until browned, then stir in taco seasoning and a splash of water. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Warm the Tortillas — Heat tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave until soft and pliable.
  3. Assemble the Tacos — Spoon the seasoned meat onto the tortillas and top with lettuce, tomatoes, onions, cheese, and your favorite sauces or garnishes.
